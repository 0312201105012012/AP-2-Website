Mohammad Yamin

Mohammad Yamin was born on August 24, 1903, in Talawi, Sawahlunto, West Sumatra. He grew up in a Minangkabau family that placed strong emphasis on education and culture. From a young age, Yamin showed great interest in literature, history, and traditional arts. His talent as a writer later led him to become one of the leading figures of the Pujangga Baru literary movement.

Beyond literature, Yamin played a major role in Indonesia’s national awakening. He became actively involved in youth organizations and contributed ideas about unity and identity. During the Youth Pledge Congress of 1928, Yamin was known for helping develop the concepts that emphasized one homeland, one nation, and one language—Indonesia.

After the period of the Youth Pledge, Yamin continued working in politics, cultural studies, and government. He served in various official positions, wrote important books on Indonesian history and civilization, and consistently advocated for national unity. Mohammad Yamin passed away on October 17, 1962, leaving a legacy as one of Indonesia’s most influential thinkers and cultural leaders.
