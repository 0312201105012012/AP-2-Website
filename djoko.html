Djoko Marsaid

Djoko Marsaid was a young Indonesian nationalist from Java who became known as one of the important representatives of Jong Java during the early independence movement. Born in the early 1900s, he grew up in a period when the spirit of unity among Indonesian youth was starting to rise. As a student, Djoko was active in youth organizations that encouraged cooperation between young people from different ethnic and cultural backgrounds.

His most notable contribution was his participation in the historic Youth Congress of 1928. During the congress, Djoko worked closely with leaders such as Soegondo Djojopoespito and Mohammad Yamin to discuss the idea of “One Nation, One People, and One Language—Indonesia.” Although he was not as widely known as some of the other figures, his involvement played an important role in shaping the atmosphere of unity that led to the Sumpah Pemuda (Youth Pledge).

After the congress, Djoko continued supporting youth movements and promoting national awareness among students of his generation. Even though historical records about him are not as complete as other figures, his dedication to the ideals of unity and nationalism remains an important part of Indonesia’s history.
