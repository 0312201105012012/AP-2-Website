Soegondo Djojopoespito

Soegondo Djojopoespito was born on 22 February 1905 in Tuban, East Java. Growing up during the Dutch colonial era, he became active in youth groups that aimed to strengthen cooperation among Indonesian students. His leadership skills developed early, especially when he joined organizations that encouraged unity across ethnic and regional backgrounds.

Soegondo’s most important contribution was his role as the Chairman of the Second Youth Congress in 1928. Under his leadership, the congress ran in an orderly and organized manner, allowing young representatives from different regions to discuss and express their hopes for a united Indonesia. The congress eventually produced the historic “Sumpah Pemuda” (Youth Pledge), which declared one homeland, one nation, and one language—Indonesia. Soegondo is remembered as the central figure who guided the discussions and ensured the success of this milestone.

Throughout his life, Soegondo remained involved in journalism, education, and political activities. He often encouraged young people to be disciplined, educated, and united in their efforts for national progress. After Indonesia gained independence, Soegondo continued serving in government institutions and public organizations. He passed away on 23 April 1978 in Yogyakarta, leaving behind a legacy as one of the key figures who shaped Indonesia’s national identity and youth movement.
